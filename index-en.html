Legend

  • Blue blot - the vaccinated
  • Yellow blot - the unvaccinated
  • Red blot - the infected
    • Basic reproduction number
    The number of people one person can, on average, infect during the infectious period. The higher R0, the farther and faster the infection spreads. For more, try the Wikipedia, for example.
    Infectious period
    Time period, within which the sick can spread the infection. I assumed that one is contagious during the first n days of their illness, beginning the day after getting infected. After that period, one will still be represented by a red blot, but won't be able to spread it any further.
    Vaccination ratio
    Simply, how many people are vaccinated.
    Vaccine's effectiveness
    They do say that vaccines aren't one hundred percent effective, don't they? Effectiveness is simply a chance that an infection, all else being in its favor, will smash its nose on the intended victim's immune system.

    The model: the area is divided into square fields — think chessboard. Every field by default contains ten people. Every tick of the clock, every person kisses (has the opportunity to infect or be infected by) everyone living on his square and on its immediate neighbor squares. He starts spreading it the day after catching it, and continues to do so for the preset number of days. As long as he's contagious, the chance of spreading it is constant, calculated basing on the R0, the infectious period, and how many people live in a single cell.
